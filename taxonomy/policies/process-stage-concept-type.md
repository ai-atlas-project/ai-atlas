# Process Stage Concept Type

Status: draft policy / under review / not canonical

Source issue: [#20 — Review Process Stage concept type and hierarchy-level policy](https://github.com/elvistudio/ai-atlas/issues/20)

## Purpose

Review `Process Stage` as a possible concept type and define when process-stage concepts may appear in deeper taxonomy levels.

## Current decision

- `Process Stage` is experimental and under review.
- It is not a public canonical AI Atlas concept type.
- Do not add it to `taxonomy/concept-types.md` yet.
- Existing structured nodes should not be mass-changed.

## Examples under review

- `Surface Realization`
- `Content Planning`
- `Sentence Planning`
- `Lexicalization`
- `Aggregation`

## Level guidance

A process stage may be considered for Level 3 only when it is:

- stable;
- broad;
- field-recognized;
- useful for understanding field structure;
- not merely an implementation substep.

Narrow stages and implementation substeps should wait for Level 4 or deeper, or be represented through relations or explanatory notes.

## Current candidate decisions

- `Surface Realization` remains `Method` for now, with Process Stage noted as a possible future treatment.
- `Content Planning` and `Sentence Planning` require focused placement review before structured node creation.
- `Lexicalization` and `Aggregation` are likely Level 4 or deeper.

These decisions are draft review positions, not canonical taxonomy changes.

## Open questions

- Can a process stage be a canonical Level 3 node?
- When should a stage be typed as Method, Technique, Component, or Process Stage?
